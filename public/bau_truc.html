Bau truc, ninh thuan Visual
afterthought

Bàu Trúc pottery stands between two poles of materialism. One appreciates the realistic substance—the earth, the craft, the hands shaping it, while the other critiques how material pursuits mold our reality. Pottery is a meditative act, and the reddish-brown hue recalls centuries of Champa soil. What provides life to an object life is arguably the intention behind it, but without nurturing the hands that form and shape the clay, how long can such a life last?

Must everything we create become materialist?

I don’t think so. I wonder if something exists in between, how do we preserve its dignity so it can stand, however shakily, on its own?
