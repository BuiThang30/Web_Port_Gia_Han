You become what you make

I now turn to materialism in the more commonly understood sense. Frankly, Bàu Trúc pottery has never been the first name that comes to mind when speaking of Vietnamese ceramics. Most people think instead of the refined, porcelain-like pieces of Bát Tràng that reflect East Asian aesthetics. Bàu Trúc’s reddish-brown, rustic pottery thus remains niche, gradually becoming unknown even to many Vietnamese in a globalized age. Because their craft brings little profit, Champa artisans must constantly balance survival with preserving their ancestral heritage. I am not Cham myself, yet seeing the struggles inclined me to learn how to hold and sell such pieces of nature, and how to commodify it to appeal to a wider, and more general sensibility.

I wrestled with such a thought for a long time, unsure if my line of thinking was the correct way forward. Have I always been this materialistic?
