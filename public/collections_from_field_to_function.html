From field to function

Sandy and dark at first, Bàu Trúc clay fades to gray, then erupts in the kiln into a chili-red glow. I tested its crystallizability, learning when to press more earth into the form, and when to carve against its stubborn surface to communicate my spontaneity. By starting with basic forms, I built the Little Champa to reflect Ninh Thuận’s nature, made of matter, and is a substance of reality.

Figure 1.1 is a vase I carved with crabs, fish, shrimp, and lotus blossoms, the same life that stirs in the ponds and rivers of Ninh Thuận. On one side, I attempted raised carvings distinguishable in Champa bas-reliefs, letting the lines flow freely as if nature and spirit were moving through my hands.

Figure 1.2 came from deep incisions and ridges, gestures that reminded me of horizons, constellations, and the steady rhythm of a heartbeat. Inspired by the Cham tale of the goddess Pô Nagar, born of sea foam and clouds, who shaped the earth and its people, I wanted the clay to hold something more than utility, to breathe with cosmology and faith.
